Redesign this catalog website with the following goals and constraints. The site is a self-service channel selection tool for crypto marketing campaigns. Users arrive already warm — they know the company, they just need to pick channels, build a bundle, and generate a checkout message for Telegram. The site must feel fast, opinionated, and human-built — not templated.
Remove or rewrite:
Delete the "How it works" three-step section entirely. Warm leads don't need it. If you keep it, reduce it to one line of text inline near the channel grid, not its own section.
Replace "Everything you need to know" with a real headline that has attitude. Something blunt and direct that sounds like a human who knows crypto wrote it.
Rewrite every FAQ answer to be half the length and twice as direct. Cut any sentence that restates the question.
Remove decorative section labels like "SHORT-FORM CONTENT" in all-caps — they add visual noise without adding information.
Channel cards — overhaul:
Add average views per video and engagement rate to every card. These are the numbers buyers actually care about.
Add a one-line audience descriptor per channel, e.g. "Skews US retail investors, heavy altcoin interest." Make it feel like insider knowledge, not a category tag.
Add a subtle "Most picked" or "High engagement" badge to one or two standout channels. Make it feel editorial, not algorithmic.
Make the selected state of a card feel significantly different — border color change alone is not enough. Consider a checkmark, a background shift, and a change to the add button label.
Bundle mechanic — make it felt:
Add a persistent sticky bar or inline counter that shows live bundle progress as the user adds channels. It should say something like "Add 2 more channels — unlock $300 off" and update in real time. The discount should feel earned, not discovered at checkout.
When a bundle tier is unlocked, give the user a small moment of feedback — a color change, a brief animation, something that rewards the action.
Checkout message:
Make the generated checkout message section the most polished part of the site. Show a preview of exactly what will be copied — channel names, services selected, total price with discount applied. Add a single large "Copy to Telegram" button. This is the conversion point, treat it like one.
Copy and tone:
Rewrite all body copy to be shorter, more direct, and with a slight edge. The audience is crypto-native and moves fast. They distrust anything that sounds corporate or over-explained.
Replace "Pick from our crypto-native YouTube channels, bundle them for automatic discounts, and reach thousands of real investors" with something that leads with the outcome, not the process.
Visual:
Increase whitespace between the channel cards and the pricing section. The page currently feels compressed.
Make the homepage stat numbers (7 active channels, 900K+ subscribers, 100+ campaigns) larger and more prominent. These are credibility signals, treat them that way.
If there is a hero visual with floating channel cards, either make it interactive or remove it. Static decorative UI elements that mimic functionality erode trust.
Keep the dark theme and red accent color. Do not change the overall color scheme.
